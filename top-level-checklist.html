Golf Simulator Setup Guide

  1. Assess Your Space:
    • NOTE: The measurements for the space need to be finalized/confirmed before purchase of the screen and side guards
    • This link will be handy for space considerations
    • Ensure your garage has enough space. Typically, you need at least 10 feet in width, 15 feet in depth, and about 10 feet in height, but check the specific requirements of the simulator you plan to purchase.
    • Consider any obstacles that could interfere with your swing, such as low ceilings or hanging fixtures.
  2. Select a Simulator:
    • Current selection: Mevo+ - pro package if aftermarket software is desired
    • Choose a simulator that fits your budget and needs. Popular options range from high-end professional systems to more budget-friendly home models.
    • Research features like accuracy, software compatibility, and additional hardware requirements.
  3. Purchase Necessary Equipment:
    • Tentative list of most items can be found here
    • List with many of the prices can be found here
    • Depending on your chosen simulator, you'll need a launch monitor, projector, impact screen, hitting mat, and potentially a gaming computer or tablet.
    • Ensure the projector and screen resolution are compatible for optimal display quality.
  4. Prepare the Environment:
    • Install proper lighting to avoid casting shadows or glare on the screen.
    • Consider adding insulation or heating if you plan to use the garage year-round.
  5. Install the Simulator:
    • Follow the manufacturer's instructions for setting up the launch monitor, screen, and projector.
    • Ensure the alignment is correct between the hitting area and the screen for accurate results.
  6. Set Up Software:
    • Install the simulator software on your computer or device.
    • Connect the system according to the instructions, ensuring all components are properly linked and configured.
  7. Test and Calibrate:
    • Conduct initial tests to make sure everything is working correctly.
    • Calibrate the system to ensure shot accuracy and adjust settings as needed.
  8. Create a Comfort Zone:
    • Consider installing a comfortable seating area or golf bag storage.
    • Add items like a putting green or chipping net if space allows for additional practice.
