Golf Simulator Setup Guide

  1. Assess Your Space
    • NOTE: Finalize measurements before purchasing the screen and side guards.
    • Helpful link for space considerations
    • Ensure your garage has at least 10 feet in width, 15 feet in depth, and 10 feet in height based on the simulator's requirements.
    • Check for obstacles that might interfere with your swing.
  2. Select a Simulator
    • Current selection: Mevo+ - pro package (for aftermarket software)
    • Choose a simulator based on your budget and requirements.
    • Research features like accuracy and software compatibility.
  3. Purchase Necessary Equipment
    • Find a tentative list of items here.
    • Price listings can be found here.
    • You’ll need various items depending on the simulator: monitor, projector, screen, mat, and possibly a computer or tablet.
    • Check that the projector and screen resolutions are compatible.
  4. Prepare the Environment
    • Install proper lighting to avoid screen glare.
    • Consider insulation or heating for year-round use.
  5. Install the Simulator
    • Follow the manufacturer's setup instructions.
    • Ensure correct alignment between the hitting area and the screen.
  6. Set Up Software
    • Install necessary software on your device.
    • Follow instructions to connect all components properly.
  7. Test and Calibrate:
    • Conduct initial tests to make sure everything is working correctly.
    • Calibrate the system to ensure shot accuracy and adjust settings as needed.
  8. Create a Comfort Zone:
    • Consider installing a comfortable seating area or golf bag storage.
    • Add items like a putting green or chipping net if space allows for additional practice.
  9. Safety Measures:
    • Install protective netting if there’s a risk of wayward shots causing damage.
    • Make sure your garage door is secure and that no vehicles or fragile items are in the hitting area.
